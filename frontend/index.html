Valuation Methodology

This tool uses the following valuation methods to estimate the intrinsic value of stocks:

1. FCFE (Free Cash Flow to Equity)

Cash available to shareholders after operating expenses, reinvestments, and debt payments. Discounted at the required return on equity.

FCFE = Net Income + Depreciation − CapEx − ΔWorking Capital + Net Borrowing
2. FCFF (Free Cash Flow to Firm)

Cash available to all capital providers. Discounted at WACC to get enterprise value, then subtract net debt for equity value.

FCFF = EBIT × (1 − Tax) + Depreciation − CapEx − ΔWorking Capital
3. Justified P/E (Gordon Growth Model)

Theoretical P/E ratio based on payout ratio, growth rate, and required return. Multiplied by EPS for intrinsic value.

Justified P/E = (1 − b) × (1 + g) / (r − g)
4. Justified P/B (Residual Income Model)

Theoretical P/B ratio based on ROE, growth rate, and required return. Multiplied by book value per share for intrinsic value.

Justified P/B = (ROE − g) / (r − g)
5. Weighted Average

Final value is a weighted average of selected models. By default, equal weights are assigned to all selected models.

These valuations are based on historical data and user-defined assumptions. They should be used as one of many tools in investment analysis, not as sole decision-making criteria.
